The Tool

Pathway Visualizer allows you to investigate all the pathways associated with 2338 different diseases.

Simply select a disease and a list of pathways associated with it will be displayed.

Select a pathway and it will be visualized within a force-directed graph.

Clicking on the pathway's ID will link you to its WikiPathways page.

The Graph

Nodes represent gene products or metabolites.

More information on the nodes can be accessed by double clicking on them.

Arrow-lines represent directed interactions between the elements of the pathway.

Arrow-heads alone pointing at a node signify self-regulation.

Pros & Cons

Pros:
-Easy visual identification of hub nodes.
-Quickly scan through all pathways associated with a disease.
-Quick access to WikiPathways and other databases.
Cons:
-Some components of some pathways might not be represented.
-Graph does not distinguish between different compartments.
